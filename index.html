About
project

Industry Agriculture and agro-industry

Our project is a modern platform that simplifies the search and sale of goods in the agroindustry. The main objective of the platform is to create a convenient and transparent space for interaction between participants of the agricultural market.

Project Goal To create a mobile application for agro-trading

Our goal is to develop a mobile application that will simplify agro-trading processes, making them accessible and convenient for all market participants. We aimed to create a platform that unites sellers and buyers of agricultural products, allowing them to interact effectively, conclude transactions and receive up-to-date information on goods and prices.

Problem
Statement

[01]

Lack of a centralized platform for interaction between producers, buyers and suppliers.

There is a clear problem with coordination between the key players in the market - producers, buyers and suppliers. The lack of a unified platform leads to chaotic communications, fragmented processes and the inability to build effective collaboration. Manufacturers struggle to find the right buyers, buyers face problems finding reliable suppliers, and suppliers cannot respond quickly to market demands. This fragmentation reduces the efficiency of transactions and increases costs for all parties.

[02]

Problems with price transparency and availability of information on supply and demand.

In many markets, there is a lack of clarity in pricing, leading to mistrust between market participants. Buyers are often unaware of actual market prices, and producers and suppliers lose the opportunity to offer their services or goods at competitive rates. In addition, information on supply and demand is incomplete or unavailable, making it difficult to plan and make strategic decisions. All of this reduces the overall efficiency of the market and increases risks for all parties.

[03]

Long timeframes for closing deals

The closing of deals is often delayed by the need for multiple negotiations, negotiating terms and compromises. Long timeframes lead to slower product turnover, lower profits and the risk of missed opportunities. In a highly competitive market, companies cannot afford to waste time waiting for deals to close, which encourages them to look for faster and more convenient solutions.
